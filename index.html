Evolving Trends in EV Adoption

Forecasting EV sales using Hybrid LSTM-GBRT Model: A Comparative Analysis of Machine Learning Approaches


The time is right for electric cars - in fact the time is critical.
- Carlos Ghosn

Abstract

This research paper presents a comprehensive analysis of machine learning models for predicting future sales of electric vehicles (EVs) in the global market, with a specific focus on Europe, India, and overall market dynamics. The study compares different machine learning approaches, including Linear Regression, Support Vector Regression (SVR), Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), and the hybrid LSTM-GBRT model. Through an evaluation of their respective errors, the hybrid LSTM-GBRT method demonstrates superior prediction accuracy, making it the preferred model for forecasting EV sales, specifically Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs), over the next five years.

By leveraging the strengths of the LSTM model in capturing temporal patterns and dependencies, and the GBRT model in refining predictions based on residual errors, the hybrid LSTM-GBRT approach offers superior predictive capabilities in forecasting EV sales. The paper aims to present the forecasted sales figures for the specified locations, providing valuable insights into the future trajectory of EV sales and serving as a vital resource for stakeholders in the electric vehicle industry.

The findings highlight the changing dynamics of the EV market, with India exhibiting a high growth rate in BEV sales, positioning it as a key player in the global EV landscape. Despite its current low contribution to global EV sales volume, India is expected to experience a significant rise in its contribution by 2027. This underscores the need for adequate infrastructure and resources to meet the growing demand and align with global EV trends. Europe, on the other hand, maintains a strong presence in the global market, with consistent contributions to both BEV and PHEV sales. Europe's emphasis on sustainable transportation and robust charging infrastructure positions the region as a leader in the adoption of electrified vehicles.

Historical EV Sales

Historical electric vehicle (EV) sales have been influenced by various factors such as consumer sentiments, government incentives, technological advancements, and charging infrastructure availability. Efforts have been made to forecast EV sales based on methods like Principal Component Analysis- General Regression Neural Network (PCA-GRNN) and historical sales data analysis. These forecasting models provide a comprehensive analysis of the factors influencing EV sales, including historical sales patterns, market growth rates, and external factors such as government incentives and policies. The research paper also highlights the importance of accurate and up-to-date market data, open access to relevant data, and collaboration among researchers, manufacturers, and policymakers for more accurate and reliable predictions, supporting the sustainable growth of the EV sector .

The findings from the research paper indicate that India is exhibiting a high growth rate in battery electric vehicle (BEV) sales, positioning it as a key player in the global EV landscape. Europe maintains a strong presence in the global market, with consistent contributions to both BEV and plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV) sales. The research paper emphasizes the need for adequate infrastructure and resources to meet the growing demand and align with global EV trends, underscoring the changing dynamics of the EV market and the potential for significant growth in India's contribution to global EV sales volume by 2027.

Overall, historical EV sales have been shaped by consumer sentiments, technological advancements, government policies, and market competitiveness, with a focus on reducing CO2 emissions and addressing the challenges posed by climate change and air pollution.

Future EV Sales

The research paper provides valuable insights into the future trajectory of electric vehicle (EV) sales, serving as a vital resource for stakeholders in the electric vehicle industry. The hybrid LSTM-GBRT model is shown to be superior in predicting future EV sales, and the forecasted sales figures for electric vehicles in Europe, India, and the global market are provided.

The research paper highlights the changing dynamics of the EV market and the potential for significant growth in India's contribution to global EV sales volume by 2027. The forecast indicates a substantial growth potential, with the BEV market share projected to increase to 3.23% by 2027. In contrast, Europe's contribution to the global BEV market is expected to decline, plummeting to a mere 22.40% of the global market by 2027. This decline is not solely attributed to Europe's market conditions but also influenced by the remarkable growth of BEV markets in countries like India and China, which are poised to surpass Europe's market share by 2027.

The research paper emphasizes the significance of utilizing machine learning models for accurate predictions of future EV sales. By leveraging these models, stakeholders, manufacturers, and policymakers can make informed decisions, develop appropriate strategies, and align their efforts with the evolving market dynamics. The findings contribute to a comprehensive understanding of the market landscape, guiding stakeholders in navigating the complexities of the EV industry and fostering sustainable growth in the future.
